Nigerian Politics Quiz

Nigerian politics is a dynamic and often dramatic landscape, shaped by a mix of ethnic diversity, historical legacies, and evolving democratic institutions. Here's a quick overview to get you oriented:

Structure of Government

Nigeria operates as a federal presidential republic, meaning:

  • Executive: Led by the President, who serves as both head of state and government.
  • Legislative: Bicameral National Assembly—Senate and House of Representatives.
  • Judiciary: Independent courts, with the Supreme Court at the top.

Major Political Parties

  • All Progressives Congress (APC): Currently dominant, with President Bola Tinubu at the helm.
  • People’s Democratic Party (PDP): Former ruling party, now the main opposition.
  • Labour Party (LP): Gained traction recently, especially among youth and urban voters.
